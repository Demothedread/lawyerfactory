Smith v. Tesla, Inc. - Complaint

1. PARTIES

Plaintiff JOHN SMITH ("Plaintiff") is an individual residing in Los Angeles County, California.

Defendant TESLA, INC. ("Tesla" or "Defendant") is a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business in Palo Alto, California, doing business throughout the United States, including California.

2. JURISDICTION AND VENUE

This Court has original jurisdiction over this action pursuant to 28 U.S.C. § 1332 because the matter in controversy exceeds $75,000 and is between citizens of different states.

Venue is proper in this District pursuant to 28 U.S.C. § 1391 because a substantial part of the events giving rise to the claim occurred in this district.

3. FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

On or about March 15, 2024, Plaintiff purchased a 2024 Tesla Model 3 from Tesla's authorized dealership in Los Angeles, California.

The vehicle was marketed as having advanced autopilot features and being safe for daily driving.

Within two weeks of purchase, Plaintiff experienced multiple defects including sudden acceleration, battery drain, and touchscreen unresponsiveness.

Plaintiff brought the vehicle to Tesla service centers on three separate occasions, but Tesla failed to repair the defects.

4. CAUSE OF ACTION: BREACH OF CONTRACT

Plaintiff realleges and incorporates by reference paragraphs 1 through 3 as if fully set forth herein.

Plaintiff and Tesla entered into a contract for the purchase of the vehicle, which included an implied warranty of merchantability.

Tesla breached this contract by failing to deliver a vehicle that conformed to its warranties and was fit for its intended purpose.

As a direct and proximate result of Tesla's breach, Plaintiff has suffered damages including the cost of repairs and diminished value of the vehicle.

5. PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Honorable Court enter judgment in favor of Plaintiff and against Defendant as follows:

  1. Compensatory damages in the amount of $95,000;
  2. Punitive damages according to proof;
  3. Pre-judgment and post-judgment interest;
  4. Costs of suit and reasonable attorney's fees;
  5. Such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper.
